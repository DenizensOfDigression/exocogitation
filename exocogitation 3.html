Exocogitation (n.)

/ɛksoʊˌkɒdʒɪˈteɪʃən/ [exo- (external) + cognition + -ation (process)]

The cognitive process of extending one's mental capabilities through systematic integration of external information resources and technological tools.1

Operational Definition

See: Extended Mind Thesis (Clark & Chalmers, 1998)

Strategic leverage of digital resources and collective knowledge as active components in problem-solving processes.2

Cognitive Science Perspective

A form of extended cognition where external systems become functional components of mental processes.3

Skill Components

1. Strategic query formulation
2. Information validation
3. Contextual synthesis
4. Application integration
5. Source evaluation
